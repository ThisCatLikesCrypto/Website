1. GPU Mining software - NBMiner (.com)

NBMiner is a great choice for mining software, especially for people with Nvidia LHR cards.

It has an easy-to-understand interface while mining, with updates about the mining process, as well as stats of your card. NBMiner is flexible in which cards you choose to use, supporting both AMD and Nvidia cards. 

There is a LHR unlocking feature for Nvidia cards, which is useful as Nvidia have locked the hashrate while mining Ethereum to only about half of its capability, however NBMiner 'unlocks' the cards to about three-quarters of its mining capacity. While it is annoying that some of the lock still works, it is better than nothing, and the boost in hashrate can earn you tens, hundreds, or even THOUSANDS more over the long term.

However, it should be noted that NBMiner takes a 2% mining fee while, isn't huge, is about double some other miner's fees.

It uses the Windows Command Line to tell it what to do, just like most other mining software. There is a guide for this here .

 

 

Pros:

-Easy-to-understand interface

-Lots of flexibility

-LHR unlocking

 

Cons:

-Relatively high fee (2%)

-Runs on command line, however some programs that have proper GUIs can charge more in fees

 

 

 

 

 

 

2. CPU mining software (And Pool)  - CryptoTab Browser (Link)

CryptoTab Browser, true to its name, is actually a Web Browser with built-in mining functionality, designed to make mining easy for the masses.

CryptoTab is a really easy-to-use CPU miner which is actually a browser based on Chromium, which Google Chrome is also based off of. 

CryptoTab mines Monero (XMR), and the hashrate on this CPU miner can be higher than with a traditional CPU miner, however the fees are quite high (3%).

CryptoTab has an amazing referral system where you can invite people to use CryptoTab, and earn 15% for their earnings, and this keeps going down to 10 levels, however the commission gets smaller every time (and the refferal system isn't what makes the most money for the average person, just the mining :-))

CryptoTab also acts as a pool, so you do not have to find one as well as your mining software.

This site pays in Bitcoin, and the minimum withdrawal is a tiny 1000 satoshi! 

The site often takes up to 5 days to process your withdrawal, however they do pay. They are currently on manual withdrawal processing however they are working on making it automatic.

The support is not that fast, however they do get back to you eventually.

-Easy-to-use

-Amazing referral system

3. GPU mining pool - Prohashing.com

Prohashing is a great multi-coin pool, supporting LOADS of payout coins

Prohashing (.com) is an amazing pool, in which you can set payout percentages. These allow you to lets say, mine Ethereum, but get paid in 70% Litecoin and 30% Bitcoin Cash. This is a great way to directly invest in the coins that you want to with little to no work on your part.  

Payouts are free depending on the threshold you set, otherwise you pay the network transaction fee.

There is also charity mining, where you donate a % to a charity.

Prohashing supports a variety of mining algorithms, including Ethash, Etchash, Scrypt, SHA-256, and loads more.

Pros:

-Great user interface

-Multi-Coin payouts 

-Charity Mining

Cons:

-Sometimes glitches and it says you have multiple workers

-Sometimes USD withdrawals can take a while

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

4. Staking software - Guarda (.co/m)

Guarda is actually a wallet, however I cover the wallet aspects in a different  article , and cover the staking aspect now

Guarda works well as a staking wallet, picking the safest and lowest-fee staking pools when you click 'stake'. It is much easier than looking up deposit addresses, and going through lengthy claiming processes. You can do it all at the click of a button.

If you are looking for a specific pool, they have a list under 'Advanced Settings' once you have clicked 'Stake' where you can click the pool you want if they support it.

You can stake many coins, including Ethereum, Tron and Zilliqa. 

Pros: 

-Lots of Coins and Pools

-Easy to use interface

Cons:

-Sometimes takes a while to load the staking information or to load the network fee
