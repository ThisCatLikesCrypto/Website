What is AI?

Narrow AI (both simple and advanced)

Simple Narrow AI excels at specific tasks. Imagine it as a specialised helper that's really good at one thing. For instance, it could be an AI recommending products on Amazon, suggesting videos on YouTube, or even identifying images, fonts, or handwriting. These AIs are like focused experts—they do their job well, but they're not versatile. Creating and training them is relatively straightforward, and anyone with the right knowledge can code one. More Advanced Narrow AI, on the other hand, aims to mimic human responses. Think of chatbots or image generators. These sophisticated AIs learn from vast amounts of data, attempting to replicate human-like behavior. They’re harder to create, but they operate in a similar way. So, while simple narrow AIs are like specialized tools, advanced narrow AIs strive to help us with a huge variety of things.

Future AGI

Artificial General Intelligence, or AGI, seeks to create machines that mimic or emulate human intelligence and behavior. These systems possess the ability to learn, reason, and apply their intelligence to solve diverse problems. This is because it is not limited to a single domain or task. Instead, it exhibits a broad spectrum of cognitive abilities, allowing it to tackle various challenges. AGI can transfer knowledge and skills from one context to another. It doesn’t merely excel in a predefined set of tasks but can generalize its understanding to new scenarios. This also means that they are extremely adaptable and act like super-humans. However, the development of AGI remains a subject of ongoing debate. Researchers and experts differ in their predictions regarding the timeline for achieving AGI. Some believe it could happen within years or decades, while others argue it might take a century or longer. There’s even a minority view that AGI may never be fully realised. Despite the challenges, AGI holds immense promise. It could revolutionise fields such as healthcare, robotics, natural language understanding, and scientific research. However, ethics and safety are crucial as we navigate the journey toward creating machines with human-like intelligence.
